Learn More about Recycling

Every day, we are producing more and more waste each day. A lot of it may or may not be recyacble. Learn a little about what can be recycled and what can not.


What You Can Recycle.

Paper and Cardboard


It should be common knowledge that paper and cardboard can be recycled. One thing to keep in mind tho is to make sure that paper and cardboard is kept clean of any food or general dirtiness. If you have a pizza box with some grease, then it is also good to be placed into the yellow recycling bin.

Cans and Aerosols


Cans and aerosols can most definitely be recycled due to them being made up from the metal Aluminium. These means items such as spray oils and personal hygiene products can be placed along with all your other recyclables.

Hard Plastic


Hard plastics such as yogurt containers and butter containers can be recycled. Other plastics such those used for fruits and vegetables can also be recycled. Any item made of plastic that can return to its original shape after being scrunched can also be recycled.

Glass Bottles and Jars


Glass bottles and jars are just like paper and cardboard and can 100% be recycled and placed into your yellow recycling bin. Remember once again to make sure all glass bottles and jars are washed out and cleaned before chucked into the yellow bin.

Household plastic bottles


Household plastic bottles such as those used for cleaning products and other hygiene products can also be recycled. For products that have had harsh chemicals included, make sure to empty them before placing them into recycling.

What You Can't Recycle.

Food Scraps


Food scraps cannot be placed alongside other recyclables in your yellow bin. Food scraps have their own particular way of being recycled. These food scraps should be put into composters to allow for later use in gardens.

Soft Plastics


Soft plastics are ones that can easily be scrunched smaller and not return to their original state. These different forms of soft plastics can sometimes have their ways of making their way into the hands of people how they can recycle them. You can sometimes take soft plastics to grocery stores.

Recycling in Plastic Bags


Many people collect and store their recycling in their kitchen bins before moving them to the yellow bin. Sometimes these are taken the yellow bins but remember to take them out of soft plastic garbage bags as they are not recyclable. If they are in a bag, they can not be sorted for recycling.

Polystyrene


Polystyrene is something that can not be included in the yellow recycling bin. If you have a cardboard box on your hand with polystyrene, the polystyrene can be taken out, and the cardboard can be flattened and put into the yellow recycling bin. You can also check with local councils as they may offer disposal of polystyrene.

Clothing


Clothing can have many lives, but they can not be included in your yellow recycling. Clothing can damage and ruin the recycling process and make it difficult to recycle other products. Clothing can be recycled by taking them to charity locations and second-hand stores.
